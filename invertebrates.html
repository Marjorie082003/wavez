Masters of the Deep

Invertebrates dominate the ocean's most extreme environments, from hydrothermal vents to the abyssal plain. These resilient creatures have evolved extraordinary adaptations to survive crushing pressures, freezing temperatures, and complete darkness.

Many deep-sea invertebrates exhibit gigantism, with species like the Japanese spider crab reaching 3.7m across, or live for centuries like the 500-year-old Greenland shark.
